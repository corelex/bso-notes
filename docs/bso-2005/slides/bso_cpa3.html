3. BSO and CPA (3)

Another scenario:

  1. A lexicographer analyses the corpus patterns for "drink" and sees the sentence: "he drank beer". He clicks "beer" and up pops the familiar list.
  2. He finds the type [[Beverage]] and decides that the type is fine, but that only a subset of its lexical entries is relevant. Say, "beer' and "ale" are fine, but "ginger ale" and "lager" are not fine, but they are good beverages, and of use in other patterns. So the lexicographer creates a pattern like this
    drink_3: [[Person]] drinks [[Beverage]] {"beer", "ale"}
  3. BSO gets notified automatically of this action, possibly by adding two entries to a table associated with the BSO entries:
    entry type pattern role
    beer Beverage drink_3 object
    ale Beverage drink_3 object

    These entries suggest to a BSO editor that "beer" and "ale", of type [[Beverage]] are rather useful entries with a usefull type and that they should not be changed lightly. CPA should be consulted or warned when the BSO types are changed.
