2. BSO Ontology

There are three major types: Event, Entity and Property. Event and Entity are both subdivided according to the natural, functional, complex distinction. We will for now ignore the Properties.

Entities

Natural kinds are things like "rock", "tiger" and "stick". They are what they are. Functional entities are things like hammer, door and knife, they are artifacts made for a particular reason. They're distinguished from natural kinds by having a telic role. Complex entities lead double lives, for example, "turkey" is both a food and an animal.

Events

Events are split the same way as entities. The type of event is determined by its arguments: the most complex argument determines the kind of event. So an event with a natural kind argument and a functional argument is itself functional.

Role inheritance

  1. multiple inheritance is possible (do we set a limit of 2 or 3?)
  2. simple default inheritance (one supertype overwrites the other)
  3. non-monotonic (subtypes can remove restrictions of roles, but cannot remove the roles themselves).
