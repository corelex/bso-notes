3. BSO and CPA (2)

Here's a simple scenario:

  1. A lexicographer analyses the corpus patterns for "drink" and sees the sentence: "he drank beer"
  2. He clicks "beer" and up pops a list from BSO with all types and supertypes for "beer". For each type, a list of entries can be displayed.
  3. He finds the type [[Beverage]] and decides that that type, with its entries, captures a normal use of "drink", and that the following pattern can be created:
    drink_2: [[Person]] drinks [[Beverage]]
  4. BSO gets notified automatically of this action, possibly by adding two entries to a table associated with the BSO types:
    type pattern role
    Person drink_2 subject
    Beverage drink_2 object

    These entries suggest to a BSO editor that [[Person]] and [[Beverage]] are rather useful types and that they should not be changed lightly. CPA should be consulted or warned when the BSO types are changed.
