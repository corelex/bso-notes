2. BSO Ontology - Consistency

Consistency of types and entries is enforced in the sense that types and entries may not refer to non-existing types.

Entries do not need to express all roles that are expressed on their types (see entry_P below, which does not express the #theme role). 

[[Type_X]]  
  #externalArgument = [[Type_Y]]
  #theme = [[Type_Z]]

'entry_P'
  type = [[Type_X]]
  #subjectRole = #externalArgument

'entry_Q'
  type = [[Type_X]]
  #subjectRole = #externalArgument
  #objectRole = #theme
  #ppRole1 = #goal **

Also, we will allow for an entry to express roles that are not defined on its type (see entry_Q above). Such a situation is sub-optimal and will be flagged in the browser (here indicated with a double star). But outruling it is too draconian and will hinder development of the ontology.
